Step 1: Identify Your Day Master

1

Find Your Core Identity

The Day Master (日主) is the Heavenly Stem of your Day Pillar — it represents you. This is the most important character in your entire chart.

Look at the top character of your Day Pillar (the third pillar from the left). This is your Day Master.

The 10 Day Masters

💡 Quick Interpretation Tip

Yang Day Masters are more outward, active, and expressive. Yin Day Masters are more inward, receptive, and intuitive.

Step 2: Understand Your Four Pillars

2

What Each Pillar Represents

Your chart has four pillars, each covering a different life period and aspect:

  • Year Pillar (年柱): Ancestry, early childhood (ages 1-15), your roots and family background
  • Month Pillar (月柱): Youth and young adulthood (ages 16-30), social environment and parents' influence
  • Day Pillar (日柱): Core adulthood (ages 31-50), your true self, spouse/partner, and current life focus
  • Hour Pillar (时柱): Later years (ages 50+), children, legacy, and spiritual growth

Step 3: Count Your Five Elements

3

Analyze Element Distribution

Each character in your chart corresponds to one of the Five Elements. Count how many times each element appears:

Element Reference

  • Wood (木): 甲, 乙, 寅, 卯 — Growth, creativity, flexibility, new beginnings
  • Fire (火): 丙, 丁, 巳, 午 — Passion, energy, visibility, transformation
  • Earth (土): 戊, 己, 辰, 戌, 丑, 未 — Stability, nurturing, groundedness, reliability
  • Metal (金): 庚, 辛, 申, 酉 — Structure, clarity, precision, determination
  • Water (水): 壬, 癸, 亥, 子 — Wisdom, adaptability, depth, introspection

What Element Balance Means

  • Abundant element: A natural strength — this energy comes easily to you
  • Weak element: An area requiring conscious development
  • Missing element: A gap that may manifest as life challenges or lessons to learn

⚠️ Important Note

Earthly Branches also contain "hidden stems" — secondary elements that affect the balance. Our calculator automatically includes these in your element count.

Step 4: Check Element Relationships

4

Generating and Controlling Cycles

Elements don't exist in isolation — they interact through two primary cycles:

Generating Cycle (相生) — Support

Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood

Each element nourishes the next. If your chart has Wood and Fire, Wood supports Fire — your creativity feeds your passion.

Controlling Cycle (相克) — Challenge

Wood → Earth → Water → Fire → Metal → Wood

Each element controls another. If your chart has strong Wood and weak Earth, Wood may "overwhelm" Earth — your growth energy might destabilize your need for security.

Step 5: Understand Your Luck Cycles

5

The 10-Year Periods That Shape Your Life

BaZi's most powerful prediction tool is the Luck Cycle (大运) — 10-year periods that shift your elemental focus. Your chart interacts differently with each cycle.

When a Luck Cycle's element matches or supports your Day Master, that decade tends to be favorable. When it clashes, you may face challenges that spur growth.

Example Interpretation

Let's say your Day Master is Yang Wood (甲) and you're entering a Water Luck Cycle:

  • Water nourishes Wood (generating cycle) ✓
  • This 10-year period supports your natural energy
  • Expect growth, opportunities, and feeling "in flow"
  • Good time to start new ventures or take calculated risks

Putting It All Together: A Reading Example

Here's how to synthesize everything into a complete reading:

  1. Day Master: "I'm Yang Fire — I'm naturally expressive, warm, and visibility-seeking."
  2. Element Balance: "I have strong Fire and Wood, but weak Water. I'm creative and passionate but may struggle with introspection."
  3. Missing Element: "No Metal in my chart — I might find structure and discipline challenging."
  4. Luck Cycle: "I'm in an Earth cycle. Earth drains Fire (I'm a Fire person), so this decade requires more effort but builds lasting foundations."
  5. Practical Advice: "Focus on systems, routines, and patience. Success comes through steady work, not flashy moves."
